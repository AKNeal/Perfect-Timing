Egg & Cheese Mug

⭐ Easy
🌡️ Microwave ⏱ 2 min

Components

  1. Microwave-safe mug
  2. 2 large eggs
  3. 2 tbsp shredded cheddar cheese
  4. Salt & pepper to taste

How to Make It

  1. Crack 2 eggs directly into a microwave-safe mug.
  2. Whisk eggs with a fork until yolks and whites are fully combined.
  3. Microwave on HIGH for 60 seconds; stir at 30 seconds.
  4. Top immediately with shredded cheddar and season with salt & pepper.
  5. Let stand 30 seconds until cheese melts, then serve.
Contains: egg, milk. Keep eggs refrigerated. Cook to 160°F internal temp.

Air Fryer Bacon & Egg Bites

⭐⭐ Intermediate
🌀 Air Fryer ⏱ 12 min

Components

  1. Silicone muffin cups (6-cup tray)
  2. 4 strips cooked bacon, crumbled
  3. 6 large eggs + 2 tbsp milk, whisked
  4. ¼ cup shredded cheddar
  5. Salt, pepper, garlic powder

How to Make It

  1. Preheat air fryer to 300°F for 3 minutes.
  2. Place silicone cups in air fryer basket.
  3. Divide bacon crumbles evenly among all 6 cups.
  4. Pour egg mixture over bacon, filling each cup ¾ full.
  5. Top each cup with a pinch of shredded cheese.
  6. Cook at 300°F for 10–12 minutes until eggs are set and no longer jiggly.
  7. Cool 1 minute before removing from cups.
Contains: egg, milk, wheat (bacon). Internal egg temp must reach 160°F. Serve 3 bites per order.

Air Fryer Breakfast Potatoes

⭐⭐ Intermediate

Components

  2. ½ cup diced bell pepper (mixed colors)

Air Fryer French Toast Sticks

⭐⭐ Intermediate
🌀 Air Fryer ⏱ 10 min

Components

  1. 4 slices thick-cut bread, cut into 3 sticks each
  2. 2 eggs + ¼ cup milk (batter)
  3. 1 tsp vanilla extract
  4. 2 tbsp cinnamon sugar (mixed)
  5. Maple syrup for serving

How to Make It

  1. Whisk eggs, milk, and vanilla together in a shallow bowl.
  2. Dip each bread stick into the batter, coating all sides; let excess drip off.
  3. Roll each soaked stick in cinnamon sugar.
  4. Preheat air fryer to 375°F for 2 minutes.
  5. Arrange sticks in basket in a single layer; do not overlap.
  6. Cook at 375°F for 8 minutes, flipping once at 4 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  7. Serve with a small cup of warm maple syrup on the side.
Contains: wheat, egg, milk. Serve immediately — sticks go soft within 10 minutes. Yields 12 sticks per batch.

Overnight Steel-Cut Oats

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🍲 Slow Cooker ⏱ Prep night before · 8 hr cook

Components

  1. 1 cup steel-cut oats
  2. 2 cups whole milk + 2 cups water
  3. 1 apple, peeled and diced small
  4. ¼ cup toasted pecans (toast day before)
  5. 2 tbsp honey per bowl
  6. 1 tsp cinnamon, pinch of salt

How to Make It

  1. Spray slow cooker insert with non-stick spray the night before service.
  2. Combine oats, milk, water, cinnamon, salt, and diced apple in slow cooker.
  3. Set slow cooker to LOW; cook 7–8 hours overnight (start by 10 PM for 6 AM service).
  4. Stir vigorously in the morning — oats will have thickened; add splash of milk if too thick.
  5. Portion into bowls using a 1-cup scoop (yields ~6 servings).
  6. Top each bowl with 1 tbsp toasted pecans and drizzle 2 tbsp honey.
Contains: oats, milk, tree nuts (pecans). Pre-toast pecans in 350°F oven for 8 min. Oats hold on WARM setting up to 2 hours.

Breakfast Casserole Cup

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🔥 Oven ⏱ 35 min

Components

  1. 1½ cups frozen hash browns, thawed and pressed dry
  2. ½ lb breakfast sausage, cooked and crumbled
  3. 6 eggs + ¼ cup milk, whisked (custard)
  4. 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar
  5. Salt, pepper, onion powder

How to Make It

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Spray a 6-cup muffin tin generously.
  2. Press 2–3 tbsp of thawed, dried hash browns into each muffin cup to form a crust shell.
  3. Bake hash brown crusts alone for 10 minutes until beginning to crisp.
  4. Layer 1 tbsp sausage crumbles into each cup on top of the crust.
  5. Pour egg custard over sausage, filling each cup to ¾ full.
  6. Top each cup with a generous pinch of cheddar.
  7. Bake at 375°F for 20–22 minutes until eggs are set and cheese is bubbly.
  8. Cool 3 minutes; run a knife around edge before releasing from pan.
Contains: egg, milk, wheat, soy. Internal temp must hit 165°F. Makes 6 cups per batch. Holds up to 2 hrs in warming oven at 170°F.

Cinnamon Roll Bake

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🔥 Oven ⏱ 30 min

Components

  1. 2 cans (12.4 oz each) Pillsbury cinnamon rolls
  2. 2 tbsp melted butter
  3. 1 tbsp cinnamon sugar
  4. Included cream cheese frosting (both cans)

How to Make It

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Butter a 9×13 baking dish generously.
  3. Quarter each cinnamon roll into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Arrange pieces in a single loose layer across the dish — don't pack tightly.
  5. Drizzle melted butter over pieces and dust with cinnamon sugar.
  6. Bake at 350°F for 22–25 minutes until golden on top and cooked through center.
  7. Warm both containers of frosting (microwave 20 seconds) and drizzle over hot bake.
  8. Scoop into serving cups while still warm; ~8 portions per batch.
Contains: wheat, milk, egg, soy. Use Pillsbury Grands for extra-soft result. Serve within 45 minutes of baking.

🍳 Stovetop ⏱ 6 min

Components

  1. 3 oz Oregon Chai concentrate (or equivalent)

  3. Pinch of ground cinnamon (garnish)

Single-Cup Pour-Over

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🍳 Stovetop / Kettle ⏱ 5 min

Components

  1. Pour-over dripper + carafe or mug
  2. 1 paper filter (size #2)
  3. 18g freshly ground coffee (medium-fine grind)
  4. 300ml water at 200°F (just off boil)

How to Make It

  1. Place filter in dripper; rinse with hot water to remove paper taste, then discard rinse water.
  2. Add 18g ground coffee to filter; tap to level.
  3. Bloom: pour 36ml water in slow circles; wait 30 seconds until coffee expands.
  4. Pour remaining water in steady slow circles, center outward, over 2–3 minutes.
  5. Wait for drip to stop completely before removing dripper.
  6. Serve in 12 oz cup with requested cream/sweetener on the side.
Grind fresh per order — pre-ground deteriorates within 15 min. Total brew time should be 3–3.5 min. Too fast = weak; too slow = bitter.

Matcha Oat Milk Latte

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🍳 Stovetop ⏱ 6 min

Components

  1. 2 tsp ceremonial-grade matcha powder
  2. 2 oz hot water (175°F — NOT boiling)
  3. 8 oz oat milk, steamed to 150°F
  4. 1 tsp honey or agave (optional)

How to Make It

  1. Sift matcha powder into mug to prevent clumping.
  2. Add 2 oz water at 175°F (not boiling — damages matcha flavor).
  3. Whisk vigorously in a W-motion with bamboo whisk for 20 seconds until frothy paste forms.
  4. Heat oat milk on stovetop to 150°F, stirring; froth with handheld frother.
  5. Pour steamed oat milk over matcha paste, stirring gently as you pour.
  6. Sweeten with honey if requested; serve immediately.
Dairy-free, vegan. Use ceremonial-grade matcha only — culinary grade is bitter. Water MUST be under 180°F or matcha turns bitter.

▲ D-06

Scratch Chocolate Chip Cookies

⭐⭐⭐ Expert
🔥 Oven⏱ 25 min

Components

  1. 1 cup (2 sticks) softened butter
  2. ¾ cup white sugar + ¾ cup brown sugar
  3. 2 large eggs + 2 tsp vanilla extract
  4. 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  5. 1 tsp baking soda + 1 tsp salt
  6. 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

How to Make It

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line baking sheets with parchment.
  2. Cream butter and both sugars until light and fluffy (2–3 min by hand or mixer).
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time; add vanilla.
  4. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt in separate bowl; gradually fold into wet mixture.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips until evenly distributed.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons onto parchment 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake 9–11 minutes until edges are golden — centers should look slightly underdone.
  8. Cool on sheet 5 minutes before transferring; cookies set as they cool.
Contains: wheat, egg, milk, soy. Pull at golden edges — overbaking makes them hard by morning. Makes ~48 cookies. Store in airtight container up to 3 days.
